Koji Hirai

Hi! My name is Koji Hirai, an aspiring engineer. I am currently studying Electrical Engineering at the University of California, Irvine.

Through various experiences and exposure to the vast range of engineering disciplines, I have ultimately learned that they are interconnected. Everything works together from a robotic arm, the circuits, and the software to control it all. In college, I am most interested in Electrical Engineering as the ability to use the microscopic formation of silicon in integrated circuits to control something much larger is truly mindblowing. I am most interested in electronics because of how much electronics surround us.

During my time in high school, I have participated in many engineering activities. One of the most significant was Gael Force Robotics. In my Junior year, I was one of the team captains where I successfully led our team to develop new strategies and techniques in our robot design. Although our season was cut short, I am proud of the anti-static design strategy that helped us optimize our performance by preventing electrostatic damage (See More in Projects Page). As the Electronics Designer and Flight Test Coordinator for the sanitation drone project (Project TERSUS), I helped design the electrical system and integrated all of the electrical components. I was also the Vice President of the Environmental Sustainability Club, where I structured the project group system allowing new projects to be supported by club resources. I also assisted with conducting air quality research and led the development of underwater vehicles.
