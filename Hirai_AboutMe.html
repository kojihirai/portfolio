Koji Hirai

Hi! My name is Koji Hirai, an aspiring engineer. In the fall, I will be studying Material Science and Engineering at the University of California, Irvine.

Through various experiences and exposure to the vast range of engineering disciplines, I have ultimately learned that they are interconnected. Everything works together from a robotic arm, the circuits, and the software to control it all. In college, I am most interested in Electrical Engineering as the ability to use the microscopic formation of silicon in integrated circuits to control something much larger is truly mindblowing. I am most interested in electronics because of how much electronics surround us. However, in the longer term, I am interested in pursuing patent law as it allows me to be involved across engineering fields and at the forefront of engineering development.

During my time in high school, I have participated in many engineering activities. One of the most significant was Gael Force Robotics. In my Junior year, I was one of the team captains where I successfully led our team to develop new strategies and techniques in our robot design. Although the season was cut short, I am proud of the anti-static design strategy that helped us optimize our performance by preventing electrostatic damage (See More in Projects Page).. Currently, I serve as the Public Relations/Event Director and as the Electronics Designer in the sanitation drone project (Project TERSUS). I am also the Vice President of the Environmental Sustainability Club, where I conduct air quality research and develop autonomous underwater vehicles.
