Solar Hydrogen Vehicle Project

Duration: November 1st, 2018 - November 15th, 2018
Course: Principles of Engineering (POE)
Objective: To build a car that is powered by solar cell or hydrogen cells
Type of Project: Group
Rubric: Here

In this project, my group and I were tasked to create a vehicle made from VEX parts and run off solar or hydrogen cells. I was one of the main builders of the car and tried to make the car as light as possible. Effects of Static Electricity and Prevention in VEX Robotics

Duration: September 2019 - December 2019
Course: N/A
Objective: Research the theory behind static electricity and prevent build up in VEX Robotics.
Type of Project: Individual
Rubric: N/A

Early in the robotics season as captain, I found that one of the main problems I and most other teams had with their robot was the fact that static discharge in the robot kept damaging the electrical ports on the microcontroller, and killed the circuits inside the motors. I decided to research how and why static electricity builds up in our robots by learning about the theory and characteristics of static electricity. I also research common industry practices on preventing electrostatic discharge. Through this process I was able to develop a new method to apply in VEX robotics. After the implementation of the new design modifications, our robot never suffered electrical damages from static discharge. I not only learned about static electricity itself, but I was able to learn about design practices used by engineers in the real world to combat these problems.

P1.2.7 Circuit Design Project

Duration: October 12, 2020 - October 21, 2020
Course: Digital Electronics (DE)
Objective: Create a control system using combinational logic circuits
Type of Project: Individual
Rubric: Here

In this project, I was tasked to create a functional system which had at least 3 inputs and 1 output. I decided to create a railroad junction point where 2 tracks join together into 1 single track. My original design was to use combinational logic to create a simple traffic light system for the tracks where when a train was approaching on 1 track, the other track's lights would be red. However, I realized that I could potentially include the actual junction into consideration for the circuit. I decided to implement a flip flop which can store a value in its memory, to store the direction in which the junction was facing. This allowed the track traffic light to change its colors based on the direction of the junction. For example, if a train was approaching on Track A, but the junction was pointing Track B, then the circuit would automatically change the traffic light on Track B to red, Track A to green and also change the junction to face Track A. This project allowed me to notice that systems that seem complex such as a railroad junction point, can be created with a combinational logic circuit. I learned the power of combinational logic and how it enables us to create various systems throughout our lives.

Texas Instruments Electronics Online Challenge 2021

Duration: September 15, 2020 - December 8, 2020
Course: N/A
Objective: Disassemble and analyze electronic device's internal components and functionality within the overall system
Type of Project: Team
Rubric: N/A

In this project, I decided to disassemble a BaoFeng 2 way radio that operates within the Amateur Radio frequencies. I was interested in the low, affordable cost of this radio despite having the ability to operate on two distinct frequencies. As I disassembled the device, I noticed the simple internal circuit board with minimal components. As I analyzed the the integrated circuit chips and other electronic components on the circuit board, I realized that this radio used a technology called software defined radio or SDR. This was a modern type of radio that used software to modulate and demodulate the radio signals, or in other words, rather than building a circuit to control the radio, much of the process was done by a processing chip and software. The SDR technology significantly simplified the radios, which traditionally used various capacitors, inductors and other components to tune the frequencies. The simplification allowed for companies with less technological experience or expertise to build radios like this. However, despite the SDR nature of this particular radio, there were still many capacitors and inductors especially near the antenna used for transmission and receiving. This made me realize the complexity of working with radios. Today, radios seem like outdated technology, especially with the introduction of cell phone and cellular networks. However, the WiFi, Bluetooth and cellular communication that we rely on in our everyday lives are still based on the same technology. Without radio technology like the ones seen in the radio I disassembled, modern communication systems may not have existed in our lives today.

P2.3.5 PLD Design Project

Duration: March 4, 2021 - March 13, 2021
Course: Digital Electronics (DE)
Objective: Create a display using a Programmable Logic Device (PLD)
Type of Project: Individual
Rubric: Here

In this project, I was tasked to create a circuit using a Programmable Logic Device (PLD). I was required to have at least 4 inputs, 5 outputs, and 16 different states. I created a display using a seven-segment display, which would display "Ready 9 8 7 6 5 4 3 2 1". The purpose of the circuit was to substitute for a camera's timer function, which is often difficult to know when the photo will be taken. Other applications include a rocket launch counter. I began by creating a truth table which was then inputed into a logic converter which created the circuit's logic. This was then uploaded to the PLD to complete the project. Through this project, I learned the significance of Programmable Logic Devices, which significantly reduces the time required to create a combinational logic circuit. There is no need to use individual integrated circuits leading to higher efficiency and compact form factor.
