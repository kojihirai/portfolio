Robotics Education and Competition Foundation 2018 Electronics Online Challenge

Duration:September 18th, 2017 - January 12th, 2018
Course: N/A
Objective: To disassemble electronic devices and analyze the internal components.
Type of Project: Group
Rubric: N/A

In this project, my group and I had to choose a consumer electronic to disassemble and write a report on the finding. I had taken apart electronics in the past but never had to analyze those components and its functions. We picked an Amazon Kindle Paperwhite to disassemble because we wanted to know how the unique screen functions, and how the tablet overall works. My role in the project was to research the Integrated Chips used and find a role in the tablet's overall function. I also researched the basic electronic components such as capacitors, resistors, diodes, oscillators to find out their functions. I was able to learn in-depth about electrical components and their applications through this project. After the research, I helped organize the information into an organized document of findings.

Solar Hydrogen Vehicle Project

Duration: November 1st, 2018 - November 15th, 2018
Course: Principles of Engineering (POE)
Objective: To build a car that is powered by solar cell or hydrogen cells
Type of Project: Group
Rubric: Here

In this project, my group and I were tasked to create a vehicle made from VEX parts and run off solar or hydrogen cells. I was one of the main builders of the car and tried to make the car as light as possible. Since the car receives very minimal voltage to run the motor, the amount of force it could output was much less. This made weight a key factor for speed and the ability to move 1 meter. The car also had to support 4 different configurations of solar and hydrogen cells which made the design even more difficult. We used the lightest components such as VEX plates which were thin and small. We also used a speed gear ratio since the motor rotated at a slow speed and we wanted to make the car move faster. Through this project, I learned the engineering challenges when building vehicles powered by renewable energy sources because of the low amount of power they produce.

Aerodynamic Design Project

Duration: January 8, 2019 - January 28, 2019
Course: Principles of Engineering (POE)
Objective: To build a rocket that flies the longest distance
Type of Project: Group
Rubric: Here

For this project, we were tasked with building a paper rocket that flies the longest distance. The constraint we had was that we could only use paper-based products and tape. This makes it harder to make durable rockets that can withstand the high pressure during the launch since the rockets were air powered. Although this was a group project each member had to create 2 rocket designs. My original design had 3 fins and a long and thin cone. However, the paper used for the body of the rocket got crushed and plugged the barrel. This makes the rocket explode at the test. For my next iteration, I used tape on the inside to prevent the issue and changed the fin to a smaller aerodynamic design. My second rocket went over the entire length of the football field. From this project, I learned the rationale behind the design for an arrow by using a similar design for my rocket. I also learned the importance of the center of gravity when building flying devices.

At first, we noticed that the tutorial app was built for a phone-sized screen and was not able to scale to the tablet we were using. Another issue we noticed was that the bird movement seemed very unnatural. The first improvement we made was to fix the screen scaling of the app so it would be full screen on tablet devices. We not only resized the spikes but also improved the randomization of the spike locations. Another improvement we made was the movement of the bird. Image Manipulation Project

Duration: March, 23 2020 - March, 30 2020
Course: Computer Science Principles (CSP)
Objective: Use Python Imaging Library and create an algorithm to modify images according to the client's request.
Type of Project: Partner
Rubric: Here

In this project, my partner and I created an algorithm for a client that wanted to add a frame that incorporated and reflected the interests of the client (art). We decided to add elements of abstract art using simple geometric shapes and frames inspired by abstract artists, Picasso and Kandinsky. In order to work together effectively, we created separate sub files while we experimented with new functions and features to make sure we don't interfere with each other. We began by adding the basic shapes to the frame and then worked on adding more details. Through the development of this project, I learned more about using computer programming to change images systematically rather than using traditional photo editing software.
